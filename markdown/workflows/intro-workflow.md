# Intro Nexus Workflow
Welcome to the Roman Research Nexus, an open platform which brings data from NASA's flagship telescopes into the hands of everybody. Whether you're getting your start in citizen science, completing course work, or just want to look at some new images from the Nanace Roman Space Telescope, this is the place to start.

This workflow has preselected tutorial notebooks that are best suited for people either outside of just beginning their time in the field of astronomy. 
These tutorial notebooks will teach the process by which one can access, filter, and visualize Roman data.

- [**Jupyter Notebook Tutorial)**](../../markdown/jupyter.md)

  Start here if you haven't already, explore the basics of working in Jupyter and learn all the skills you'll need to be able to run the notebooks.

## Workflow Overview

- [**Roman Mission Introduction**](../../markdown/roman-intro.md)

  Before we start utilizing the data, lets learn about where the data comes from. Explore this mission and get and idea of the type of technology and instrumentation we'll be working with. Gain a background on the purpose and details of the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope Mission, as well as its mission goals.

- [**Data Discovery and Access**](../../notebooks/data_discovery_and_access/data_discovery_and_access.ipynb)

  Now that you know what Roman is, lets figure out how to find its data. Due to the vast amount of data which we will be receiving from the telescope data from Roman will utilize cloud based storage through the MAST system. So, this notebook will teach how to retreive WFI Roman imaging as well as other data from other active missions (HST, TESS, and JWST) from MAST. After this notebook you'll be able to search for any celestial object that the Science Operations Center has data on and allow you to take that data you found onto the next notebooks.

- [**Working With ASDF**](../../notebooks/working_with_asdf/working_with_asdf.ipynb)

  Now that we have found our data lets figure out how to open and handle it. The Roman Space Telescope utilizes ASDF data, so in this notebook you will learn a plethora of tools and functions allowing you to search, load, and read ASDF. Additionally learn about the process by which Roman Data is formated and categorized as you explore ASDF metadata. By the end of this notebook you'll have an understanding of the structures and functions related to the data produced by Roman enabling you to take on new data yourself.

- [**Data Visualization**](../../notebooks/data_visualization/data_visualization.ipynb)

  You've learned about what the data is and how you are able to access it, now lets start to work with it. In this notebook you will take the methods you learned to access data and apply it to methods of visualization so that we'll be able to look at the images and data Roman is providing. Additionally you will learn about forms of interfenece which can occur with imaging and data collection. Explore different filters and methods of visualization to gain all the tools you'll need to look at all the amazing images from thr Roman space telescope.

- [**Footprint Visualization**](../../notebooks/footprint_visualization/footprint_visualization.ipynb)

Good, now you have the tools to work with the nexus, lets explore the kinds of science and analysis that one can accomplish with Roman. In this notebook you will learn about the various survey's that the Roman Space telescope will complete during its mission. Furthermore, you will be able to utilize the visualizations of the survey footprints to see if, when, and how an astronomical source may be observed by Roman. With this final tutorial in the workflow, take the step to your own citizen science and find the sources you want to use all the tools you just learned to explore.

  

## Caveats and Limitations
- This workflow primarily targets induviduals who are not intially fully endowed with the knowledge of astronomy, by providing a curated selection of tutorials utilized by Astronmers. Additionally providing explanations of concepts when possible to make certain ideas more accessible.
- It is highly recommended to explore the resources linked throughout the notebooks in order to gain a fuller grasp of the ideas and concepts discussed in this workflow.
