# Intro to the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope

Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope is NASA's next Flagship mission. The Roman observatory is equipped with Wide Field Instrumentation as its primary instrument, as well as, the Roman Coronagraph Instrument. Roman’s Wide Field Instrumentation has the capabilities of observing 59% if the sky at anytime. So, when compared to previous observatories, like Hubble Space Telescope, the scale of data collection possible will be magnitudes larger than what has been possible prior. For example Roman will image 50 times the area of what Hubble was able to accomplish in its 30 years of operation, in Roman's first 5 primary years of mission operation. 

During its mission, Roman will conduct different surveys of the sky including the Galactic Bulge Time-Domain Survey, High-Latitude Time-Domain Survey, High-Latitude Wide-Area Survey, and the Galactic Plane Survey. Each of the surveys will contribute to our understanding of the Universe, as well as serving Roman's primary missions of studying and discovering Exo-Planets, and measuring Dark Energy. 

Data from Roman will be stored and available to users via the Roman Archive, with WFI data stored in Advanced Scientific Data Format (ASDF). Instructions on how to access and utilize the Roman data is available throughout the Roman Nexus' Workflows and Tutorial Notebooks.
